Our Purpose

GreenRater is a digital platform designed to improve how urban greenspaces are evaluated and understood. By combining research-based criteria with community input, GreenRater creates a more efficient and inclusive evaluation process for parks and natural spaces. The application empowers both urban planners and community members by providing a structured scoring system that highlights the quality, accessibility, and overall value of greenspaces. Through this approach, GreenRater aims to support better planning decisions while giving the public a stronger voice in local environmental development.

Key Features

  • 5-category weighted greenspace scoring system
  • Detailed breakdown of each evaluation
  • MySQL database for storing and analyzing ratings
  • Public catalogue of evaluated greenspaces

About GreenScore

How We Calculate the GreenRater Score

The GreenRater score is a weighted evaluation that incorporates academic research and community perspectives. Each greenspace is scored using five criteria categories, with weights determined through a 65% community-input-based and 35% research-based analysis. This hybrid model ensures that the final score is both data-driven as well as reflective of real-world experiences and community needs.

Understanding GreenScore Categories

Each greenspace is scored quantitatively, with a numeric score (1.0-5.0) and a qualitative rating suuch as 'Poor', 'Okay', 'Fair', or 'Great'. These categories aid in translating a more abstract numeric score to a more definitive evaluation of a greenspace. View the numeric to categoric scale on the left to see how categories are assigned.
